# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This README is your starting point for all project management documentation at OctoAcme. Whether you're a new team member getting oriented or a contributor looking for a specific process, everything you need is linked below.

## Overview

OctoAcme's project management approach emphasizes a structured, transparent, and iterative process anchored around customer value and clear ownership. All projects begin with a rigorous initiation phase, where the team validates the business need, defines success metrics, aligns stakeholders, and produces a concise one-pager outlining the problem, objectives, primary stakeholders, and key milestones. Once a project moves to planning, objectives are refined into actionable backlogs, with each item carrying explicit acceptance criteria, estimated effort, and clear ownership. A risk register is established during planning to systematically identify, assess, and mitigate project risks and inter-team dependencies.

Execution and tracking are underpinned by disciplined team rhythms, including daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and sprint demos. OctoAcme standardizes workflows by leveraging project boards with clearly defined columns from backlog to done, and enforces pull request conventions: each PR should reference relevant issues, meet documented acceptance criteria, and pass required testing steps before approval. Metrics such as team velocity and project burndown are actively tracked, and blockers are escalated in a staged manner—starting with daily team triage and escalating up to sponsors for business-critical issues.

Roles and personas are clearly defined to promote accountability and enable efficient collaboration. Project Managers (PMs) coordinate delivery tasks, manage risk, and facilitate communication, while Product Managers (PdMs) own outcome definition and prioritization. Developers focus on implementation and quality and are expected to contribute throughout the lifecycle in estimation, testing, and documentation. Stakeholders, including sponsors and other teams, play an ongoing part by approving project gates and providing feedback during key communications.

Communication is both frequent and purposeful, with weekly syncs, standup meetings, and monthly stakeholder updates ensuring alignment and visibility. Quality assurance practices are embedded throughout the workflow: unit and integration testing, end-to-end smoke tests, security scanning, and manual QA are all leveraged as appropriate. Each release is subject to a checklist-driven process that covers pre-release requirements, deployment verification, and incident handling with a robust rollback playbook. Retrospectives and continuous improvement sessions close the loop, encouraging teams to turn lessons learned into actionable follow-up tasks that foster a culture of growth and excellence.
